Help & Tutorials

Step-by-step guides to get the most out of PhiXtra.

Before You Start — Checklist

Complete all three steps in Meta first. Tick each one off as you go.

Meta Business Account created and verified
You need a verified Meta Business Manager before you can use the WhatsApp API. Verification usually takes 1–2 business days — start this first.
Open Meta Business Manager ↗
Meta Developer App created with WhatsApp product added
In Meta for Developers, create a new "Business" app. Inside the app, click "Add Product" and choose WhatsApp. This unlocks the API Setup section where you'll get your credentials.
Open Meta Developer Apps ↗
WhatsApp phone number added to your WABA
In WhatsApp API Setup, add or verify a phone number under your WhatsApp Business Account (WABA). This is the number your customers will message.
Go to WhatsApp API Setup ↗
All prerequisites done — you're ready to connect your number in the WhatsApp settings page.
Step-by-Step Setup Guide

Exactly where to find each setting in Meta's dashboard.

1
Create and verify your Meta Business Account
Go to Meta Business Manager and create an account if you don't have one. Then go to Business Settings → Security Centre and submit your business for verification. You'll need to provide business documents. WhatsApp API access is gated behind this — it typically takes 1–2 business days.
Meta Business Manager ↗ Security Centre ↗
2
Create a Meta Developer App
Go to Meta for Developers and click Create App. When asked for the app type, choose Business. Give it a name (e.g. "YourBusiness WhatsApp") and link it to your Business Account. Once created, click Add Product on the app dashboard and select WhatsApp.
Meta for Developers ↗
3
Find your Phone Number ID and WABA ID
In your app dashboard, go to WhatsApp → API Setup. You'll see your test or production phone number listed. Click on the phone number to reveal the Phone Number ID. Just below the number list you'll see the WhatsApp Business Account ID (WABA ID).
→ Phone Number ID → WABA ID
Open API Setup ↗
4
Generate a permanent System User access token
The default token shown in API Setup expires in 24 hours and will break your integration overnight. Go to Meta Business Manager → Business Settings → System Users, create a System User with Admin role, then click Add Assets and grant it access to your WhatsApp App with Manage permission. Finally click Generate Token, select your app, tick whatsapp_business_messaging and whatsapp_business_management, and copy the token. This token never expires.
→ Access Token
Business Settings — System Users ↗
5
Connect your number in PhiXtra
Once you have your credentials, go to the WhatsApp settings page in this portal. Use the Connect with WhatsApp button for the easiest experience — it handles everything automatically. If you prefer to paste credentials manually, expand the "Having trouble?" section on the same page.
Go to WhatsApp settings →
Dashboard

Your command centre. See WhatsApp status, AI stats, hot leads, and customers waiting for a human agent — all at a glance.

1
Check your WhatsApp connection & plan
The green strip at the top shows your connected number, current plan, and credit balance, and lets you open the Agent Inbox or buy more credits in one click.
2
Reply to customers waiting for you
Customers who asked for a human are listed here with their last message. Click any card to jump straight into that conversation.
3
Track leads, credits and AI performance
The KPI tiles and charts below show hot leads this week, your remaining credit balance, message volume, and how often the AI hands off to a human — so you always know how your AI assistant is performing.
Conversations

Every WhatsApp conversation your AI handles appears here. Here's exactly how to find, reply to, and manage them.

1
Browse, search, and filter your conversations
The left panel lists everyone who's messaged your WhatsApp number, most recent first. Use the search box to find someone by name or number, or the filter dropdown to narrow the list to 🔴 Needs agent, Unread, Replied, or 🔥 Leads only — handy once you have more than a handful of chats.
2
Open a conversation and reply
Click a customer to open the full chat exactly as your AI assistant sees it. Type your reply in the box at the bottom and click Send — or press Ctrl+Enter — to jump into the conversation yourself. Your message goes out over WhatsApp immediately.
3
Add an emoji to your reply
Click the 😊 icon above the message box to open the emoji picker. Browse by category or use the search box at the top of the picker to find one quickly, then click it to insert it into your draft.
4
Rename a contact inline
Click the ✏️ pencil next to a customer's name at the top of the chat to give them a proper name instead of just a phone number. Type the name and click Save — it updates everywhere they appear in the portal, including Contacts.
5
Know when a customer needs you
A 🔴 red dot next to a customer's name means your AI has stepped aside and they're waiting for a human — usually because one of your Handoff Rules was triggered. Reply as normal, then click 🔴 Mark Resolved in the top right once you're done so the AI resumes handling that conversation.
Contacts

Every customer who messages you is saved automatically. Here's exactly how to add, import, search, edit, and export your audience.

1
See everyone who's messaged you
Total Contacts, Added This Week, and Named Contacts are summarised at the top, with the full list of names and phone numbers below.
2
Add a single contact manually
Click + Add Contact and fill in the Phone Number (required — include the country code, no + or spaces), an optional Display Name, and optional Notes for context like "VIP customer" or where they came from. Click Save Contact when you're done.
3
Import many contacts at once
Click Import CSV and drop in a spreadsheet with phone, name, and notes columns — only phone is required, and the importer auto-detects which column is which. If a phone number already exists, that contact is updated instead of duplicated.
4
Edit a contact's name or notes
Click the pencil icon on any row to open Edit Contact, update the Display Name or Notes, then click Save Changes. This is also where you'd jot down context for a follow-up, like "Called about bulk order."
5
Search for a specific contact
Type a name or phone number into the search box and click Search to filter the list — useful once your audience grows beyond a quick scroll.
6
Export your contact list
Click Export CSV any time to download every contact as a spreadsheet — handy for backups, bulk edits elsewhere, or importing into another tool.
Leads

Customers showing strong buying intent are listed here, automatically — here's how to read and use the list.

1
Check the KPI strip first
The three tiles at the top give you the headline numbers: 🔥 Hot leads need a reply now, ⭐ Warm leads are worth reading when you have a moment, and the third tile is your total leads identified. Every lead here was scored automatically from the conversation — you don't set anything up.
2
Filter to Hot or Warm only
Click the All / 🔥 Hot / ⭐ Warm pills just below the KPI strip to narrow the list — useful once you have more than a few leads and want to triage the urgent ones first. The active filter is highlighted in dark, and the count in brackets tells you how many you'll see.
3
Read why a customer was flagged
Each card shows tags like "High engagement" or "Initiated contact" explaining why that conversation was scored — plus a number in the top-right ring (the lead score) and how many messages they've sent. If you see Awaiting your reply, that customer's last message still hasn't been answered.
4
Follow up while interest is high
Click anywhere on a lead's card to jump straight into that conversation in Conversations, with the full chat history already loaded — see the Conversations guide for how to reply, add an emoji, or hand a conversation back to the AI once you're done.
Campaigns

A complete walkthrough for sending your first WhatsApp broadcast — from opening the page to confirming launch.

1
Open Campaigns and start a new one
From the Campaigns page, click + New Campaign in the top right. A setup panel slides in with 3 steps: Campaign Info → Recipients → Schedule & Launch. The tiles above track your total campaigns, completions, and messages sent.
2
Step 1 of 3 — name it and choose a template
Give the campaign a clear name (e.g. "May Flash Sale") so you can find it later, then pick one of your Meta-approved WhatsApp templates from the dropdown — only templates already approved in Meta Business Suite show up here. If the template has an image, video, document, or text header, a matching field appears automatically; fill it in or upload the file before continuing.
3
Step 2 of 3 — add who should receive it
Paste one phone number per line, including the country code, with no + or spaces (e.g. 2348012345678). The counter in the corner confirms how many numbers you've entered. Only message customers who've opted in to hear from your business on WhatsApp — sending to unverified numbers can get your account flagged by Meta.
4
Step 3 of 3 — review, then launch
Choose Send Now to launch right away, or Schedule to pick a specific date and time. The Review & Confirm summary repeats your campaign name, template, recipient count, and send time so you can double-check everything. Click Launch Campaign once it looks right — there's no further confirmation after that, so this is the step to slow down on.
5
Check delivery performance afterwards
Switch to the Reports tab any time after sending to see delivery results for every campaign — useful for spotting what landed and what didn't.
6
Know the difference: automatic AI messages
The Proactive Message Log further down the Campaigns page shows messages your AI sends automatically — like cart recovery nudges and order updates. These run on their own and are separate from the manual campaigns you create with the steps above.
Orders

Every order your AI assistant takes on WhatsApp lands here — here's exactly how to move one from placed to delivered.

1
See orders at a glance, then filter by status
The summary tiles show today's order count and revenue, and how many are pending, paid, or dispatched. Below that, click a status tab — Awaiting Payment, Receipt Received, Paid, Processing, Dispatched, Delivered, or Cancelled — to focus on the orders that need your attention. Click any order's reference to open its full detail page.
2
Verify payment once a receipt comes in
When a customer pays by bank transfer and submits proof, the order moves to Receipt Received. Check the transfer has actually landed in your account, then click ✅ Verify Payment — this confirms the order and automatically messages the customer that their payment was received.
3
Mark it as dispatched
Once an order is Paid or Processing, the Mark as Dispatched card appears. Fill in the courier and tracking number (both optional) and click 🚚 Mark Dispatched — the customer is automatically notified on WhatsApp, with the tracking details included if you added them.
4
Confirm delivery
Once the customer has the item in hand, open the order and click ✅ Mark Delivered. The order timeline turns fully green, the customer gets a final WhatsApp confirmation, and no further actions remain for that order.
5
Message the customer or cancel if needed
💬 Message Customer on WhatsApp is always available on the right side of an order's detail page if you need to follow up directly. Cancel Order appears for any order that hasn't shipped yet — cancelling automatically restores any reserved stock back to your catalogue.
6
Export your order data
Use Export CSV at the top of the Orders page to download the current filtered view for bookkeeping or reporting.
WhatsApp Connect

Everything on the Connect page — linking your number, checking its health, and configuring how your AI greets customers.

1
Connect your WhatsApp Business number
Click Connect with WhatsApp — a Meta popup walks you through choosing your Business Account and phone number, no copy-pasting credentials. Once linked, the Connection Status card shows your number, WABA ID, and a token-validity badge so you know at a glance if you'll ever need to reconnect.
2
No Tech Provider approval yet? Connect manually
If the one-click button isn't available for your account, expand Having trouble? Connect manually instead to paste your Phone Number ID, WABA ID, and a permanent System User access token directly — there's a built-in step-by-step guide right below the fields showing exactly where to find each one in Meta's dashboard, plus your Webhook URL and Verify Token ready to copy into Meta.
3
Share a QR code to start conversations
Click QR Code on the Connection Status card to get a scannable code that opens WhatsApp and pre-fills a message to your business — great for storefronts, flyers, or receipts. Copy the link directly or download the PNG to print.
4
Set an instant acknowledgement message
Under Agent Behaviour, type a short message — like "One moment, checking that for you..." — that's sent the instant a customer messages, before the AI finishes thinking. It reassures customers the bot is actually active. Leave it blank to disable. Click Save to apply.
5
Get personally alerted when a customer needs you
Under Alert Notifications, enter your own personal WhatsApp number (not your business number) with country code. Whenever a customer asks for a human, you'll get an instant WhatsApp message with their details and a link straight into the portal. This number also receives your daily business report. Click Save Number.
6
Know what else lives under WhatsApp
Two more items sit in the WhatsApp sidebar group you may not need right away: Message Templates is a premium add-on for cart-recovery and order-update templates — contact support to enable it if you don't see it. WhatsApp Plan shows the same subscription tiers as Credits & Billing → Subscription Plans, just accessible from here too.
Agent Inbox

The dedicated queue for conversations your AI has stepped aside from — and how to step in yourself.

1
Reply to conversations that need a human
The left panel lists every conversation currently waiting on a person — either because the customer asked for one, or one of your Handoff Rules triggered it. Click one to open the chat, type your reply in the box at the bottom, and send as normal.
2
Hand the conversation back to the AI
Once you've sorted the customer out, click ✓ Resolve & Return to AI above the chat. The AI resumes replying to that customer on its very next message — there's nothing else to configure.
3
Proactively take over a conversation
Below the handoff queue, Recent AI Conversations lists chats the AI is still handling normally. If you want to jump into one yourself — without waiting for the customer to ask or a rule to trigger — click Take Over. It moves into the queue above tagged "Taken Over" so you can reply directly.
Handoff Reports

How well — and how fast — your team responds when the AI hands a conversation over.

1
Pick a time range
Use the Last 7 / 14 / 30 / 90 days tabs at the top to change the window for every figure on the page, including the four summary tiles: total handoffs triggered, average response time, missed replies (no agent ever responded), and how many are open right now.
2
Read the day-by-day breakdown
The table shows, for each day, how many handoffs were triggered, resolved, and missed, plus the average response time — useful for spotting whether a particular day or shift is consistently slower to respond.
3
Jump straight to an open handoff
The "Open right now" tile includes an Open inbox → shortcut, and the Recent Handoffs list below shows each customer's status (Open, Missed, or Resolved) — click through to the Agent Inbox to reply.
WhatsApp Report

A 30-day birds-eye view of WhatsApp activity — separate from the agent-focused Handoff Reports.

1
Read the four headline numbers
Conversations, handoffs to agent (with the percentage of conversations that needed one), new customers, and orders placed with their total revenue — all over the last 30 days.
2
Check the daily trend and top products
The bar chart shows conversations per day over the last 14 days, so you can spot spikes around promotions or campaigns. Top products browsed on WhatsApp alongside it shows what customers are actually asking about most.

Discount Settings

Control whether your AI can offer discounts on its own, plus default and per-product discount amounts.

1
Choose who's allowed to give discounts
"Merchant Only" means the AI never discounts without your approval. "AI Then Merchant" lets your AI offer a configured discount automatically, then hand off to you if the customer pushes for more.
2
Set a default and per-product discount
The Default Discount applies to every product unless you add a Per-Product Override for specific items that need a different rate.

Payment Gateways

Accept Paystack or Flutterwave payments automatically, or add your bank details so customers can pay by transfer.

1
Connect Paystack or Flutterwave
Paste your Public and Secret API keys for whichever gateway you use, then click Connect. Once connected, your AI can send customers a real payment link to complete checkout on WhatsApp.
2
Add a bank account for transfers

AI Assistant

Customise your AI's personality and behaviour. Set handoff rules to decide when the AI steps aside for a human agent.

1
Tell your AI what you sell and how to behave
System Instruction lets you pick your business category, choose behaviours like "take orders & collect delivery details" or "never quote prices", and preview exactly how your AI will talk to customers. Changes apply instantly.
2
Decide when a human should step in
Handoff Rules let you list phrases or situations — like "speak to a human" or a visitor asking about an order issue — that hand the conversation to your team instead of the AI.
3
Keep product answers accurate
Verified Specs Settings lets you set trusted domains and custom spec types — like load capacity or warranty — so your AI only states product facts it can verify, instead of guessing.

Buy credits any time

3
Download your invoices

Manage your logo, security and plan
Use the side tabs to upload an Avatar, update Security, check your current Package Plan, edit Business Info, or set Notifications and Preferences.
